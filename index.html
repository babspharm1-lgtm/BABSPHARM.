🎓 100L Survival Pack
🎓
Welcome to Great Ife!
Everything a fresh OAU student needs to know to survive and excel in 100 level. Read this carefully — it could save your academic life!
⚠️ Things Nobody Will Tell You
🔴
Cult groups actively recruit in 100L
Be very careful of strangers offering money, favours or protection. If anyone pressures you to join a group, report to security or DSA immediately. Your life is more valuable than any group.
🔴
Carryover in 100L follows you for YEARS
One carryover becomes two, two becomes four. Take every single course seriously from day one. Do not wait till exam to start reading.
🔴
Do not skip lectures in first semester
You do not know the system yet. Some lecturers mark attendance and it counts towards your final score. Be present.
🔴
Do not be too trusting of new friends
Not everyone who smiles at you is your friend. Some people will collect your notes, your money and your time. Choose friends wisely.
🟡
Social media will destroy your GPA
Instagram, TikTok and Twitter will waste your reading time. Discipline yourself — there is time for everything but know your priorities.
🟡
Do not lend money you cannot afford to lose
University friendships and money do not always mix well. Be kind but be wise.
🟡
First semester GPA sets the tone
It is much harder to recover from a bad first semester than to maintain a good one. Start strong.
🟡
Know where the Dean of Student Affairs office is
If you have any problem — academic, personal or safety — that is where to go.
💰 Money Management Tips
💡
Budget your allowance the day you receive it
Divide it immediately: food, transport, data, books, emergency. Whatever is left is your spending money — not the other way around.
🍽
Buka area near faculties has the cheapest food
Do not eat at expensive restaurants every day. Find the affordable food spots near your faculty and become a regular.
🏠
Cook in your hostel when possible
Even simple cooking saves a lot of money over time. Rice, beans, noodles — learn to cook basic meals.
📱
Data and recharge cards drain fast — budget for them
Do not finish your data on entertainment and then have no money for important things.
💰
Always keep emergency money
Set aside a fixed amount you never touch unless it is a real emergency. Medical, transport home, urgent fees.
📚
Get your textbooks from seniors
Do not buy new textbooks immediately. Find seniors who have the same books — they often sell cheap or give for free.
⚠️
Do not spend all your allowance in the first week
The excitement of freedom will tempt you. Resist it. The semester is long.
📝 Exam Tips That Actually Work
🏆
OAU past questions are EVERYTHING
Lecturers repeat questions more than you think. Collect past questions from seniors for every course. Use BABSPHARM Cloud quiz section to practice them.
📋
Read your course outline religiously
Lecturers follow the course outline strictly. If something is not in the outline, it probably will not come in the exam. Focus your reading.
👥
Form study groups BEFORE exam period
Do not start a study group the night before exam. Form your group in week 2 or 3 and meet regularly throughout the semester.
✍️
Write legibly and structure your answers
OAU lecturers are marking many scripts. A well-structured answer with clear headings scores better than a messy correct answer.
📅
Start reading 3 weeks before exam
Not 3 days — 3 weeks. One week per week of the semester you were supposed to be reading. Emergency reading does not work at OAU level.
✅
Attempt EVERY question
Even if you are not sure, write something. You cannot score marks on a blank page. Structured guessing is better than nothing.
🎯
Know your lecturers favorite topics
Pay attention in class to what a lecturer emphasises repeatedly — those things WILL come out in the exam.
💤
Sleep before exam day
Your brain needs rest to recall information. Pulling all-nighters the night before an exam usually hurts more than it helps.
🧠 Mental Health & Wellbeing
💜
First semester is the hardest — you are not alone
Every single 100L student is struggling just like you. The ones who look confident are also scared. It gets better — I promise.
🏠
Homesickness is completely normal
Missing your family, your home, your old life is natural. Give yourself time to adjust. Call home regularly. It gets easier week by week.
🚫
Stop comparing yourself to classmates
Someone in your class will always seem smarter, richer, more confident. That comparison will destroy you. Run your own race.
🆘
If you are failing, get help EARLY
Do not wait until you have failed everything before seeking help. Talk to your course adviser, a senior student or a trusted lecturer early.
😴
Sleep is not a luxury — it is academic fuel
Your brain consolidates memory during sleep. A student who sleeps 6-7 hours and reads smartly beats the one who reads all night every night.
🙏
Your faith is your anchor — use it
Many OAU toppers credit their faith as what kept them going through the tough times. Stay connected to what gives you peace.
👥
Find your tribe
Find 2-3 people who are serious about their studies and who lift you up. Your circle determines your direction at OAU.
💪
Struggling does not mean failing
Having a bad week, failing a test or missing a deadline does not mean you are going to fail out. Recover, restrategize and keep going.
🏆
You were admitted to OAU for a reason!
Great Ife doesn't produce ordinary graduates. You are now part of a legacy of excellence. Work hard, stay focused, trust God and make your family proud! 💚
